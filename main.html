The Diary of a Young Girl
by Anne Frank

Also known as The Diary of Anne Frank, written during the Nazi occupation.

Anne called her diary "Kitty", writing letters addressed to it.

Pride and Prejudice
by Jane Austen

A romantic novel of manners depicting the British gentry.

Focuses on Elizabeth Bennet’s growth and love story with Mr. Darcy.

Moby-Dick
by Herman Melville

The epic tale of Captain Ahab’s obsession with the white whale.

A symbolic work about revenge and man vs nature.

1984
by George Orwell

A dystopian novel about totalitarian government surveillance.

Introduced terms like Big Brother and thoughtcrime.

To Kill a Mockingbird
by Harper Lee

A classic novel about racism and justice in the Deep South.

Seen through the eyes of young Scout Finch.

The Great Gatsby
by F. Scott Fitzgerald

A story of wealth, love, and the American dream in the Jazz Age.

Explores the illusion of success and happiness.
